Pork Sinigang

Sinigang is a tamarind-based soup that is known for its sour and savory taste. You can make it with either fish, pork belly, spare ribs or corned beef. My favorite is with pork spare ribs, lots of veggies, and a bed of rice.

other recipes for Sinigang:

Chicken Sinigang - Chicken Sinigangmade lightly rich and creamy with shavings of taro (gabi). It's sour, savory and loaded with vegetables. If you're looking for a great alternative to Pork Sinigang, then give this a try. It's so good!

Shrimp Sinigang - Sinigang na Hipon is a type of Filipino sour soup, wherein shrimp is used as the main ingredient. This dish also includes a variety of vegetables such as daikon radish, snake beans, okra, and eggplant. This dish is best served during cold weather. It is best enjoyed hot with a cup of white rice.

Fish Sinigang - Fish sinigang or sinigang na isda is a type of Filipino fish soup wherein fish is cooked in a sour broth together with a variety of vegetables. This version makes use of fried red snapper (also known as maya-maya).

Pork Sinigang

This recipe uses pork as the main ingredient. Other proteins and seafood can also be used. Beef, shrimp, fish are commonly used to cook sinigang. The chicken version, on the other hand, is called sinampalukang manok.

Course: Main Dish

Cuisine: A traditional Filipino Dish

Keywords: Sinigang, Pork, Sinigang na baboy

Prep Time: 10 Minutes

Cook Time: 1 Hour

Total Time: 1 Hour and 10 Minutes

Servings: 6 People

Calories: 892 kcal

Author: John Ezekiel Duran

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs pork belly or buto-buto
  • 1 bunch spinach or kang-kong
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 12 pieces string beans sitaw, cut in 2 inch length
  • 2 pieces tomato quartered
  • 1 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 2 quarts water
  • 1 piece onion sliced
  • 2 tablespoons granulated white sugar
  • 1 pack sinigang mix good for 2 liters water

Instructions:

  1. Heat the pot and put-in the cooking oil
  2. Saut√© the onion until its layers separate from each other
  3. Add the pork belly and cook until outer part turns light brown
  4. Put-in the fish sauce and mix with the ingredients
  5. Pour the water and bring to a boil
  6. Add the taro and tomatoes then simmer for 40 minutes or until pork is tender
  7. Put-in the sinigang mix and chili
  8. Add the string beans (and other vegetables if there are any) and simmer for 5 to 8 minutes
  9. Put-in the spinach, turn off the heat, and cover the pot. Let the spinach cook using the remaining heat in the pot.
  10. Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
